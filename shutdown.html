MeetingHero.AI Has Shut Down

After an incredible journey building AI-powered meeting preparation tools, I've made the difficult decision to shut down MeetingHero.AI. I'm grateful for every user who trusted me with their meeting preparation needs.

Why We Shut Down

📈 Demand Shift & AI Fatigue

When I started, there was tremendous appetite for AI tools. However, the market has shifted significantly. Users now experience "AI fatigue" and are more selective about which AI tools they adopt and pay for.

🎯 Market Fit & Value Proposition

While I had passionate users, I struggled to clearly communicate my unique value proposition. Many potential users didn't immediately understand how MeetingHero.AI differed from general AI tools.

🤖 Competition from General AI Tools

Users increasingly turned to ChatGPT and other general AI tools to achieve similar results without the integration. While my specialized approach was more efficient, the convenience of all-in-one solutions won out.

💰 AI Costs

I assumed AI costs would decrease faster than they did. While costs have come down, the rate of reduction wasn't sufficient to make my economics work at the scale I needed.

⚖️ Economics of Scale

I had many power users who significantly increased my costs, but not enough users with low to medium usage to subsidize those extra costs. This created an unsustainable unit economics model.

📢 User Acquisition Challenges

People are tired of paying for subscriptions, word-of-mouth and referrals didn't scale as expected, and my marketing and cold outreach efforts weren't successful enough to sustain the business.

💡 What I Learned

Market Timing Matters

The AI tool market shifted faster than I anticipated. Early adopters became fatigued, and new users were more selective.

Value Proposition Clarity

I struggled to clearly communicate why MeetingHero.AI was better than using ChatGPT directly for meeting prep.

Unit Economics Are Critical

Power users consumed resources disproportionately, making it hard to build sustainable unit economics.

User Acquisition Reality

Organic growth and referrals weren't enough. Paid acquisition was expensive and didn't scale as expected.
